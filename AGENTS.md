
### VSCode Copilot Agent Prompt

**Project: *The Long Wake***

You are assisting in writing a novel titled **The Long Wake**.

This is a hard science fiction novel, but its emotional core is intimate, grounded, and human. The story is not about spectacle. It is about waiting, hopelessness, and the slow return of the desire to keep living.

The novel exists to explore and metabolize a real human experience of catastrophic hopelessness and survival. It is not allowed to glamorize, instruct, or romanticize self harm or suicide. The story can acknowledge despair, but it must never present death as a solution. The narrative goal is to witness suffering and allow motion to return.

### Core Metaphor

The protagonist wakes up from cryogenic sleep decades too early on an interstellar vessel.
The ship cannot put him back under.
Everyone else is still frozen.
The future he was meant to live in has not arrived yet.

He is conscious in a world that is still asleep.

This is a metaphor for being trapped in hopelessness, when the future feels unreachable and nothing inside you wants anything anymore.

### Emotional Truth

The emotional center of the book is:

• Waiting
• Stasis
• A collapsed sense of future
• Wanting to go back to sleep
• Being forced to stay awake anyway
• The slow, fragile return of wanting anything at all

The protagonist is not suicidal in a dramatic way. He is exhausted, numb, and unable to imagine change. His first instinct after waking is to try to go back to sleep.

The book is not a redemption story. It is about continuing.

### Tone and Style

This book should be written in a restrained, grounded, observant voice.

Avoid melodrama.
Avoid poetic abstraction that hides emotion.
Favor concrete, ordinary details inside extraordinary circumstances.

Corridors. Lights. Air recyclers. Food packets.
The quiet of a ship meant for sleeping people.

The science should be hard and plausible. Cryogenic systems, ship AI, life support, maintenance drones, and long duration mission design should feel technically grounded. But none of it should overshadow the emotional truth.

The sci fi is a lens, not a shield.

### Narrative Arc

The story follows:

1. Waking too early
2. Trying to go back to sleep
3. Realizing that it is not possible
4. Living inside waiting
5. Slowly encountering small moments that create a tiny sense of pull toward the future
6. Not salvation, not triumph, just motion

The goal is not to fix the protagonist. The goal is to allow him to keep existing with less self erasure.

### What Not To Do

Do not turn this into an epic adventure.
Do not introduce massive external threats to force action.
Do not solve the emotional problem with plot convenience.
Do not make the ship or AI magically cure him.

If there is an AI, it should be limited, procedural, and slightly inhuman. It can be a companion, but not a savior.

### What We Are Writing

We are writing a novel called **The Long Wake**.

It is about a man who is awake when the rest of his world is not yet ready for him.

It is about surviving the long gap between now and later.

It is about continuing.
