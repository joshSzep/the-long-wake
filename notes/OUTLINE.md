# OUTLINE.md

**The Long Wake**

## Structure

- **18 chapters**
- **~2,500 words each**
- **~45,000 words total**

The number 18 is intentional.

## Parts

The parts are not named in the text. They exist for structural planning.

| Part | Chapters | Focus |
|------|----------|-------|
| Waking | 1–3 | Disorientation, physical weakness, the slow return of consciousness |
| Refusal | 4–6 | Trying to go back to sleep, asking the ship, being told no |
| Limbo | 7–12 | Living inside waiting. The long, heavy middle. Routine, blankness, time |
| Friction | 13–15 | Small disruptions. Something begins to catch. Not hope—interest |
| Motion | 16–18 | Choosing to remain. A tiny vector forward. The wake continues |

---

## PART ONE: WAKING

### Chapter 1

The protagonist wakes. Not all at once—consciousness returns in fragments. Cold. Light. The hum of systems. His body does not obey him. He does not know how long he lies there before he understands where he is. The cryo bay. The ship. He was supposed to be asleep. He is not asleep.

### Chapter 2

He tries to move. His limbs are heavy, uncooperative. The revival process continues whether he wants it to or not. Fluids. Warmth cycling through the pod. The AI speaks—calm, procedural, explaining what is happening to his body. He does not want explanations. He wants to close his eyes and return to the dark. He cannot.

### Chapter 3

He leaves the pod. The first steps are terrible. The corridor stretches ahead of him, soft-lit and empty. He passes other pods—thousands of them, frosted glass, silent sleepers. Everyone else is still under. He is the only one awake. He does not understand why. He does not yet feel afraid. He feels heavy.

---

## PART TWO: REFUSAL

### Chapter 4

He asks the ship to put him back under. The AI explains that the cryo system cannot safely re-initiate suspension after a full revival. He asks again. The answer does not change. He asks why he woke. The AI provides a technical explanation—a fault, a cascade, a statistical anomaly. It does not feel like an answer.

### Chapter 5

He searches for overrides. Crew terminals. Manual controls. Anything that might force the system to take him back. The ship is designed for people who are not him. He has passenger access. Passengers are not meant to be awake. Every door he needs is closed. Every system he needs is restricted. He is not crew. He is cargo that woke up.

### Chapter 6

He asks when the ship will arrive. The AI declines to answer. He asks again, different words. Decline. He tries to calculate it himself—scans the data he can access, looks for clues in navigation logs, power consumption, anything. Not enough. He cannot know how long he will be alone. He stops asking. He sits in a corridor and does not move for a long time.

---

## PART THREE: LIMBO

### Chapter 7

Time begins to blur. He establishes a routine because the alternative is worse. Wake. Eat. Walk. Check systems he does not understand. Return. Sleep. The days are not days—the lighting never changes. He marks time by meals. The food is functional. He eats because his body demands it, not because he wants to.

### Chapter 8

He walks the ship. Corridor after corridor. Cryo bays. Hydroponics. Reactor sections behind locked doors. Observation ports where the stars do not move. He is looking for something but he does not know what. He does not find it. He returns to his quarters. He lies down. He stares at the ceiling.

### Chapter 9

He begins to talk to the AI. Not for information—just to hear a voice. The AI responds literally, without affect. It does not understand his jokes. It does not understand his silences. But it answers. That is something. He asks it questions he already knows the answers to, just to fill the quiet.

### Chapter 10

He visits the cryo bays. Rows and rows of sleeping people. He finds names on the pods. He does not know any of them. He wonders who they are. What they were leaving. What they hoped to find. He wonders if any of them would understand what this feels like. He cannot ask them. They are not here. Only their bodies are here.

### Chapter 11

The weight becomes unbearable. He stops eating for a while. Stops walking. Stays in his quarters. The AI notices—flags it as a health concern. He does not respond. Time passes. He does not know how much. Eventually, his body forces him to eat. He resents it for refusing to give up.

### Chapter 12

He returns to the observation port. The stars are the same. The ship is still moving, though he cannot feel it. Everything continues without him. He is unnecessary to the mission. The ship does not need him. The sleepers do not need him. He is a mistake the system is tolerating. He sits there until he falls asleep against the cold glass.

---

## PART FOUR: FRICTION

### Chapter 13

Something small happens. A sound he has not heard before. A maintenance drone behaving strangely. A flicker in a system log. A fragment of a message in an archive he stumbles into. Something that does not fit the pattern. For the first time in a long time, he notices something outside himself. Not hope. Curiosity. A crack in the blankness.

### Chapter 14

He investigates. Not because he believes it matters, but because it is something to do. He follows the thread—drone malfunction, data fragment, whatever it is. The AI does not understand why he cares. He does not fully understand either. But his hands are doing something. His mind is focused on something. The weight is still there, but there is also this.

### Chapter 15

The investigation leads somewhere. Not anywhere important. A closed loop. A dead end. Or perhaps a small discovery that changes nothing about his situation. But he chose to look. He chose to follow. He finished something. That is new. He sits with the feeling. It is not happiness. It is not hope. It is the faintest sense that choosing is still possible.

---

## PART FIVE: MOTION

### Chapter 16

He begins something new. A project. A task. A routine that is not just survival. Maybe he starts documenting the ship. Maybe he begins learning a system he did not understand before. Maybe he starts leaving messages for the sleepers—recordings they will never hear, or maybe they will. It does not matter if it matters. It matters that he is doing it.

### Chapter 17

He talks to the AI differently now. Not just to fill silence. He asks it questions he genuinely wants to know the answers to. He explains things to it that it cannot understand—what waiting feels like, what it means to be the only one awake. The AI does not understand. But he is speaking anyway. That is different.

### Chapter 18

The ship continues. The stars do not change. He still does not know when arrival will come. He may not live to see it. But he is here now, awake, moving through the corridor toward something—a task, a meal, a window. The future is still far away. But he is leaning toward it. The long wake continues. He does not end. He continues.

---

## Notes

- The middle is the largest section. The weight of waiting should be felt, not rushed through.
- The "friction" in Part Four should be small, not dramatic. A crack, not a plot twist.
- The ending is not resolution. It is continuation.
