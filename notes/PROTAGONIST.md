# PROTAGONIST.md

**The Long Wake**

The protagonist is not a hero.

He is a man who woke up too early.

## Who He Is Emotionally

He is exhausted in a way that sleep does not fix.

He is not actively in pain, but he is buried under a weight of nothingness. The future feels closed. The past feels distant. The present feels endless.

He does not hate himself.
He does not believe he is worthless.
He simply does not feel pulled toward anything.

That absence of pull is what makes him want to go back to sleep.

## What He Wants

At the beginning of the story, he wants only one thing.

To stop being awake.

Not to die.
Not to escape forever.
Just to not have to exist in this long, empty stretch of time.

He wants cryosleep the way a person wants to close their eyes when something hurts too much to look at.

## What He Is Afraid Of

He is afraid that nothing will ever change.

That this empty, suspended state is not a glitch.
That it is what the rest of his life will feel like.

He is not afraid of the ship.
He is not afraid of the mission.

He is afraid of permanence.

## His Inner Conflict

He is caught between two truths.

The future exists.
He cannot feel it.

That mismatch creates unbearable tension.

Every system on the ship tells him there is an arrival date. His nervous system tells him there is nothing ahead.

This is what makes waiting painful.

## How He Changes

He does not transform into a different person.

He does not become confident or motivated.

He changes in one small but crucial way.

He begins to feel the faintest pull toward something.

A curiosity.
A routine.
A connection.
A memory.

It does not make him happy.
It makes him able to keep going.

## What He Represents

He represents anyone who has lost access to their future but is still alive inside time.

He is not broken.
He is out of phase.

The story does not judge him for this.

It stays with him until the phase begins to shift.

That is his journey.

Not to be fixed.

To remain.
